# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## Do's 
1. **Do audit your privacy settings:** Regularly check who can see your personal posts across platforms like Instagram and Facebook.
2. **Do showcase your projects:** Use platforms like LinkedIn or X (Twitter) to share your coding progress, GitHub repositories, and academic wins.
3. **Do follow industry leaders:** Curate your feed to follow tech companies, AI researchers, and professional organizations to stay updated.
4. **Do pause before posting:** Apply the "billboard test"—if you wouldn't want a future employer to see it on a giant billboard, don't post it.
5. **Do engage constructively:** Participate in professional groups and forums by asking good questions and offering helpful insights.

## Don'ts 
1. **Don't post offensive or polarizing content:** Avoid engaging in inflammatory arguments or sharing inappropriate media.
2. **Don't complain about your university or professors:** Venting about strict grading or assignments online leaves a highly unprofessional digital footprint.
3. **Don't share sensitive information:** Never post photos of your ID cards, passwords, or confidential university/project data.
4. **Don't ignore spelling and grammar:** Even on casual platforms, consistently poor communication habits can bleed into your professional presence.
5. **Don't forget the internet is permanent:** Deleted posts can still be screenshotted; always assume everything you post is permanent.
