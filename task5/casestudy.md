Case Study: The QR Code "Receive Money" Scam (UPI Fraud)

What the crime is: UPI payment fraud occurs when scammers manipulate victims into authorizing unauthorized bank transfers, often by exploiting the victim's misunderstanding of how the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) works.

How it typically happens (Step-by-Step): > 1. A student lists an old textbook or a piece of furniture for sale on an online marketplace like OLX or Facebook Marketplace.
2. The scammer poses as an interested buyer, urgently agreeing to the asking price without negotiation.
3. To "prove" their intent, the scammer offers to send a small token amount (e.g., ₹10) or the full payment immediately via a UPI app (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm).
4. Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a "Payment Request" link or a QR code to the student on WhatsApp.
5. The scammer instructs the student to click the link or scan the code and enter their UPI PIN, falsely claiming this step is required to receive the funds into their account.
6. Once the student enters their PIN, the specified amount is instantly deducted from their bank account and transferred to the scammer.

Who is targeted: College students, individuals selling items online, and those less familiar with the technical differences between sending and receiving money online.

Consequences: Victims face immediate financial loss, which is often difficult to recover quickly. It also causes significant stress, loss of trust in digital payment systems, and potential exposure of linked bank account details.
