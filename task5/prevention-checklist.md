# Stay Safe Online: Cybersecurity Checklist for Students

1. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money:** Your UPI PIN is only required when *sending* money or checking your bank balance. You never need it to receive funds.
2. **Always verify the receiver's UPI ID:** Before completing any online transaction, double-check the registered name that appears on your screen to ensure it matches the person or business you intend to pay.
3. **Beware of "urgent" requests from friends:** If a friend or family member asks for money urgently via social media or WhatsApp, call their actual phone number to verify their identity before sending anything. Their account may be compromised.
4. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for financial transactions:** Do not make fee payments or use banking apps while connected to free, unsecured networks at cafes, airports, or campuses. Use your mobile data instead.
5. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for your university email, social media, and any account linked to your finances.
6. **Don't click on panic-inducing SMS links:** Ignore text messages claiming your bank account, SIM card, or electricity will be blocked immediately unless you click a link. Official institutions do not operate this way.
7. **Keep your devices updated:** Regularly update your phone's OS and apps to ensure you have the latest security patches against malware.
8. **Use strong, unique passwords:** Avoid using the same password for your student portal, email, and banking. 

**REPORTING CYBERCRIME IN INDIA** 
If you or a batchmate fall victim to a cybercrime or financial fraud, report it immediately:
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** Dial **1930** (Call immediately to freeze fraudulent transactions).
